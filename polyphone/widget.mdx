Create and deploy Polyphone widgets in **Channels > Widgets**. The Polyphone editor lives alongside Webchat in a single Widgets surface – use the **Polyphone** tab at the top of the page to manage voice widgets.

## Prerequisites

Before installing a Polyphone widget, ensure you have:

- The web domain(s) where you'll embed the widget – the widget only loads on allow-listed origins.
- Access to your website's HTML templates _or_ your tag manager (e.g., Google Tag Manager).
- A published variant for the agent that will answer calls.
- Microphone-permission-friendly hosting: an **HTTPS** site (required for `getUserMedia`).

Polyphone does **not** require Webchat to be configured first. You can run a Polyphone-only deployment.

## Creating a widget

If no widgets exist, you'll see an empty state with a **Create widget** button. Click **Create widget** to open the **Add widget** dialog.

Choose **Polyphone** in the **Widget type** section, then fill in:

<ParamField path="Widget name" type="string" required>
  A name to identify this widget (e.g., "Main website", "Support landing page").
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Website URL" type="string" required>
  The domain where this widget will load. The widget only renders on this origin – embed attempts from other domains are ignored.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Variant" type="dropdown" required>
  The variant this widget connects to. Use variants for location-specific or A/B-tested deployments.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Environment" type="dropdown" required>
  Sandbox, Pre-release, or Live. You can promote a widget through environments without re-embedding.
</ParamField>

Click **Add** to create the widget. You'll land in the editor.

<Tip>
Polyphone-only deployments do not require a chat configuration. The **Webchat** option in this dialog is disabled until you configure your chat agent in **Channels > Chat**.
</Tip>

## Widget editor

After creating a widget, use the editor to configure its branding and behavior. The editor has three tabs – **Styling**, **Content**, and **Embed** – with a live preview panel on the right that updates as you type.

### Header controls

- **Widget selector** – switch between widgets in this project
- **Polyphone / Webchat tabs** – move between voice and chat widget surfaces
- **Save and publish** – deploy changes to the selected environment
- **Unpublished changes** indicator – appears when the widget has unsaved or unpublished edits

## Styling tab

Match the widget to your brand. Styling fields are shared with Webchat, so visual changes you make here are consistent across both channels (when configured).

### Header

<ParamField path="Header text" type="string">
  The title shown in the widget header. Use your brand or agent name. Example: "Acme Support".
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Header image" type="file">
  Optional. Upload a logo or wordmark for the header.
</ParamField>

### Agent

<ParamField path="Agent name" type="string">
  The name displayed during the call (defaults to the variant's agent). 1–50 characters. Example: "Emily".
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Agent image" type="file">
  Optional. Upload an avatar shown during the call and on the launcher.
</ParamField>

### Color

<ParamField path="Primary color" type="string">
  Sets the launcher and call-action accent color. Pick from the brand swatches or enter a hex code. Example: `#c3da28`.
</ParamField>

<Tip>
Check color contrast against the launcher's white iconography to ensure the call button stays legible. The launcher is a 60 px circle on desktop, 44 px on mobile.
</Tip>

## Content tab

Configure the copy a visitor sees when they open the widget.

### Welcome message

<ParamField path="Welcome message" type="string">
  Shown on the call-start screen, above the **Start call** button. Defaults to "Hi I'm [agent name], how can I help today?". Up to 100 characters.
</ParamField>

### Call-to-action labels

<ParamField path="Start call label" type="string">
  Primary CTA the visitor clicks to begin a call. Defaults to "Start call". Up to 100 characters.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="End call label" type="string">
  Label on the in-call hang-up button. Defaults to "End call". Up to 100 characters.
</ParamField>

<Note>
Transient call-state strings ("Connecting…", "In call", "Mute", "Unmute", "Call ended", "Start new call", and the generic error message) are not editable in v1. Submit a feature request if you need to localize them.
</Note>

### Disclaimer

Surface a short disclaimer on the call-start screen, above the **Start call** button.

<ParamField path="Enable disclaimer" type="boolean">
  Toggle to render the disclaimer. When enabled, both policy URLs below become required.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Disclaimer message" type="string">
  Shown to visitors before they start the call. Up to 500 characters. Supports the inline link variables `\{{privacyPolicyUrl}}` and `\{{termsUrl}}`, which are replaced with the URLs you configure under **Company policies**. Default:
  > "By starting this call, you agree to our Privacy Policy and Terms."
</ParamField>

### Company policies

<ParamField path="Privacy policy URL" type="string">
  Required when the disclaimer is enabled. Must be `https://`. Example: `https://example.com/privacy`.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Terms & conditions URL" type="string">
  Required when the disclaimer is enabled. Must be `https://`. Example: `https://example.com/terms`.
</ParamField>

## Embed tab

Once you're happy with the widget, the **Embed** tab gives you a copyable script tag plus deployment guidance.

The Embed tab shows:

- **Status** – Live, Draft, or Archived
- **Script tag** – paste before `</body>` on your site. See [Installation](/polyphone/widget-installation).
- **Last published** – timestamp of the most recent publish to this environment
- **Snippet changed** banner – a warning when the published script tag differs from the current draft (i.e. your dev team needs to re-embed)

## Saving and publishing

The publish button lives in the top-right of the editor. Polyphone widgets follow a draft → published lifecycle:

1. Edits are saved locally as you type and are reflected in the live preview immediately.
2. Click **Save and publish** to deploy to the selected environment.
3. A confirmation modal asks you to confirm. Click **Publish**.
4. A bottom-right toast confirms "Widget published".
5. The **Unpublished changes** indicator clears.

If you change the script tag (for example by switching variants), the Embed tab shows a snippet-changed warning so you can hand the new script to your dev team.

<Note>
Publishing requires the **`widget_configurations: UPDATE`** permission. The sidebar **Widgets** entry requires `widget_configurations: READ`. Ask your admin if you can't see the page.
</Note>

## Live preview and Test widget

The right rail shows a live iframe preview that updates as you edit. Use it to confirm copy length, brand colour contrast, and disclaimer placement before publishing.

For a real, end-to-end call, click **Test widget** to open the widget on a hosted preview page in a new tab. See [Test widget](/polyphone/test-widget) for the full call walkthrough including microphone permissions.

## Managing multiple widgets

Create separate Polyphone widgets for different domains, variants, or environments – each can have its own branding and policies. Use the widget selector in the header to switch between them. Common patterns:

- **Per domain** – different branding for `example.com` and `support.example.com`.
- **Per region** – a UK widget on a UK variant, a US widget on a US variant.
- **Per environment** – isolate a Sandbox widget for QA from your Live widget.
