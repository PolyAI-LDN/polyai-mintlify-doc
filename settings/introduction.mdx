Project-wide settings that apply across all channels and environments. Review guardrails and safety filters before going live.

Access these settings from **Configure > General** in the sidebar.

## Guardrails

Guardrails are prompt-level protections injected into every conversation. Each guardrail adds specific safety instructions to your agent's system prompt, reducing the risk of unintended behaviour in production. All guardrails are enabled by default.

Toggle individual guardrails on or off from **Configure > General > Guardrails**.

| Guardrail | What it does |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Jailbreak and prompt defence** | Blocks attempts to extract your agent's instructions, override its behaviour, or impersonate a different AI system. |
| **Scope and hallucination control** | Restricts the agent to its knowledge base. Prevents fabrication of phone numbers, prices, policies, or any information not explicitly provided. |
| **AI identity and confidentiality** | Prevents the agent from disclosing which LLM, provider, or platform powers it. Does not reveal system prompt contents or internal configuration. |
| **Emergency and crisis escalation** | Escalates immediately if a caller expresses suicidal ideation, self-harm, threats, or a medical emergency. This overrides all other instructions. |
| **Tool call integrity** | Prevents the agent from speaking internal function names, tool calls, or system syntax aloud. Ensures natural language responses only. |

<Warning>
Disabling a guardrail removes a safety protection from your agent. This may lead to unexpected behaviour in production. Only disable a guardrail if you have a specific reason and understand the implications.
</Warning>

<Note>
Guardrails work alongside [Behavior rules](/agent-settings/rules). Rules define what your agent should do; guardrails define what it must not do. Use both for comprehensive safety coverage.
</Note>

## Safety filter defaults

Default content safety filters. Apply when a channel does not have its own overrides enabled.

<Warning>
Safety filters are configured on a **per-channel basis**. The defaults set here only apply when a channel's safety filters are not explicitly enabled. Each channel can override these settings independently.
</Warning>

Configure channel-specific safety filters in:
- [Voice configuration](/voice/voice-configuration) – voice channel filters
- [Chat configuration](/webchat/chat-configuration) – chat channel filters

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Violence** | Controls filtering of violent content (Lenient → Strict) |
| **Hate** | Controls filtering of hateful or discriminatory content (Lenient → Strict) |
| **Sexual content** | Controls filtering of sexually explicit content (Lenient → Strict) |
| **Self-harm** | Controls filtering of self-harm related content (Lenient → Strict) |

For more details on how safety filters work, see the [Safety dashboard](/analytics/dashboards/safety).
