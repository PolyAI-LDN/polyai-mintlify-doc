Studio Assistant is the AI copilot built into PolyAI Studio. Describe what you want, and it reads your project, plans the work, and – with your approval – makes the changes on a branch you can review before merging back to `Main`.

<Note>
Studio Assistant works on top of the same building blocks you use manually – flows, topics, entities, functions, handoffs, and agent settings. Every edit lands on a **branch**, not on `Main`.
</Note>

## A typical session

The five steps you'll repeat for most work:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start from the home screen">
    The chat panel opens with a prompt input and templates grouped by intent: **Build**, **Test**, **Ask**, and **Analyze**. Pick a suggestion, edit it, or type your own request. The **Agent wizard** entry under Build kicks off a guided builder for brand-new agents from a company URL. The usage indicator next to the send button shows your current spend.

    ![Studio Assistant home screen with Build/Test/Ask/Analyze templates and the usage indicator](/images/studio-assistant/home-templates.png)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Ask about your agent">
    For exploration, the assistant reads your agent settings, flows, topics, entities, and functions, then replies with answers grounded in your project. The tools it runs (`read`, `explore`, and so on) appear inline before the final message.

    ![Studio Assistant response showing a project-specific Agent Overview for a Bella Vista restaurant agent](/images/studio-assistant/ask-mode-response.png)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Make a change on a branch">
    The first time you ask for a change, Studio Assistant creates a branch automatically and names it after the request. `Main` stays untouched while the assistant works on the branch. The branch chip replaces `Main` in the top-left for the rest of the session.

    ![Studio Assistant creating a fix-booking-flow-wiring branch before making any changes](/images/studio-assistant/branch-created.png)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Approve, revise, and review">
    Before showing a plan, the assistant may ask a clarifying multiple-choice question. Pick an option, type your own answer in the input below, or **Skip** to let the assistant choose.

    ![Studio Assistant asking a clarifying multiple-choice question with a Skip option, on the add-bella-noches-redirect branch](/images/studio-assistant/approve-flow.png)

    Once it has enough context, the assistant presents a plan card with **Approve** and **Reject** buttons. **Nothing is written until you approve.** Click the plan card to expand the full breakdown – flow changes, topic updates, transfers, voice samples, and anything else it intends to touch. From the expanded view you can write feedback for the assistant to revise the plan, then **Send** to re-plan.

    ![Expanded plan modal for a Bella Noches redirect flow with a feedback textarea and Cancel/Send buttons](/images/studio-assistant/approve-flow-revise.png)

    After you send revisions, the plan is marked **Changes requested** and your feedback is shown above the regenerated plan, so you can see what the next version is responding to.

    ![Plan card after revisions submitted: a Changes requested pill, a Your feedback block quoting the typed feedback, and the regenerated plan summary below](/images/studio-assistant/approve-flow-revised.png)

    When you **Approve**, the changes are applied to your branch (never to `Main`). Every applied edit is summarized in a **changes made** card you can expand to see exactly which steps, flows, or settings were modified, and which branch the change landed on.

    ![A single-step fix applied to the collect_phone_number step on the fix-booking-flow-wiring branch](/images/studio-assistant/change-applied.png)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Merge to main">
    The **Merge** button in the top-right opens a dialog where you can add an optional message explaining the changes, then ship the branch back to `Main`. From there, your changes flow through the normal [environments and versions](/environments-and-versions/introduction) process to Sandbox, Pre-release, and Live.

    ![Merge to the main branch dialog with an optional message typed in](/images/studio-assistant/merge-dialog.png)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Conversations and history

- Chats are saved in the **History** panel on the left, scoped to the project.
- Resume any past chat, switch between concurrent ones, or archive what you no longer need.
- The assistant remembers everything in the current chat. Follow-ups like "now do the same for the cancellation flow" work without repeating context.
- Drag in transcripts, policy docs, or screenshots to attach files. The assistant reads them as part of the request.
- Start a new chat when switching to unrelated work. Each chat carries its full history as context, so very long chats can slow responses or trigger automatic summarization.

## What Studio Assistant can work on

Studio Assistant operates on the same building blocks you can edit by hand. For any of these, it can read your current configuration, propose changes, and write the changes to a branch.

| Building block | Examples of what the assistant can do |
|----------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Flows** | Create new flows; restructure steps, transitions, and conditions; rewrite step prompts |
| **Topics** | Create knowledge-base topics from a description; update wording or scope |
| **Entities** | Define entities and their values; update entity definitions used by flows |
| **Functions** | Wire flows to existing functions; describe and create new function specs |
| **Integrations** | Configure integrations and connect them to flows |
| **Handoffs** | Add transfer-to-human steps with the right conditions and metadata |
| **Agent settings** | Set or update purpose, role, and personality |

For complex requests, the assistant breaks the work down internally and delegates to specialized reasoning steps. You don't manage these directly; they appear as plan content and tool calls in the conversation log.

## Working effectively

Three patterns matter most:

- **State the business rule, not just the action.** "Add a refund flow with a 30-day window, original payment method only" gets you a better first draft than "Add a refund flow".
- **One unit of work per request.** One flow, one topic, one set of related changes. Easier to review, faster to iterate.
- **Be specific about what to keep.** For requests that touch existing content – *"rewrite this flow"*, *"clean up the knowledge base"* – call out anything that should stay, or ask for a plan first so you can see what will be removed.

## Limits

Studio Assistant has a per-account spending limit on model calls. The amount and reset cadence (`daily`, `weekly`, `monthly`, or `total`) are configured by PolyAI on your account; internal PolyAI accounts are unlimited. The current spend and reset cadence show next to the message input.

When you hit the limit, the assistant pauses (a *Resting until tomorrow* card replaces the input) until the next reset. Manual editing in Studio is unaffected.

[Usage and limits →](/studio-assistant/usage-and-limits)

## What to expect

- **Branches only.** Studio Assistant edits the branch you're on. It never publishes or promotes between [environments](/environments-and-versions/introduction) on its own.
- **One scoped task at a time.** It works best on focused requests. For very large changes, break them into stages.
- **Business knowledge comes from you.** The assistant won't invent prices, hours, policies, or names. If those are missing, planning will surface them as questions.
- **Always reviewable.** Every change appears on a branch with a tracked-changes summary, so you can inspect, tweak, or revert it before merging.
