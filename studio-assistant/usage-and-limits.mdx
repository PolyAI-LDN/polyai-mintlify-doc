Studio Assistant has a **spending limit** to keep usage predictable. The limit is configured per account; when one is set, it's shared across everyone using Studio Assistant on the account, across all projects.

You can see your current usage from the indicator next to the message input. The tooltip on that indicator shows the reset cadence (for example, *Resets daily*).

## Spending limit

- **Scope:** per account, shared across all users and projects.
- **Cadence:** the period after which credits refresh. One of `daily`, `weekly`, `monthly`, or `total`.
- **Amount:** a USD-equivalent cap on model spend, set by your PolyAI representative. Internal PolyAI accounts are unlimited.

| Period | Behavior |
|--------|----------|
| `daily` | Credits refresh every 24 hours. |
| `weekly` | Credits refresh once per week. |
| `monthly` | Credits refresh once per calendar month. |
| `total` | A fixed lifetime allowance with no automatic refresh. |

<Note>
The amount and cadence are set on the account record. If your team needs a different limit, contact your PolyAI representative.
</Note>

## What counts toward the limit

The limit tracks the **model spend** of your interactions with Studio Assistant – the cost of running the underlying LLM calls that power planning, reasoning, and tool use.

In practice, the things that increase spend the most are:

- Long conversations with lots of context to reload each turn.
- Requests that touch many flows, topics, or entities at once.
- Iterating on the same plan many times before approving it.

## What happens when you hit the limit

When the limit is exhausted, Studio Assistant pauses until the next reset. The chat panel replaces the prompt input with a *Resting until tomorrow* card, with a **Talk to sales** link if you need more capacity.

Two ways forward:

- **Wait** – credits refresh at the next period boundary. Accounts on a `total` plan don't auto-refresh.
- **Talk to sales** – if your team consistently needs more, your account limit can be raised. Use the *Talk to sales* link on the rest card, or contact your PolyAI representative.

While the assistant is resting:

- The chat panel stays available – you can read past conversations and review plans.
- Manual editing in Studio is unaffected. You can keep building flows, topics, and other configuration the normal way.

## Other transient errors

The assistant also surfaces friendly status messages when something goes wrong outside of usage limits. These are temporary – retry with the action shown.

| Status | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| **A bit busy right now** | Studio Assistant is handling lots of requests. Give it a moment, then retry. |
| **Studio Assistant is taking a breather** | The assistant service is temporarily unavailable. Retry shortly. |
| **Something went sideways** | The assistant tripped on the way to a response. Retry. |
| **Lost my train of thought** | The response cut off mid-stream. Retry to regenerate. |
| **Already working on something** | A run is in progress. Stop it before starting a new request. |
| **Lost the connection** | The browser can't reach the assistant service. Reconnect to resume. |
| **Couldn't read your files** | An attachment expired or couldn't be read. Reattach the files. |

If any of these persist, contact PolyAI support with a timestamp and the conversation ID.

## Tips for staying within your limit

- **Ask for a plan before big work.** A single planning round is much cheaper than asking the assistant to build, then redo, then redo again.
- **Scope each request to one unit of work.** Smaller, focused requests reload less context and spend less.
- **Use the assistant for exploration too.** Read-only questions about an existing agent are cheap and don't risk wasted build cycles.
- **Approve plans early.** Long back-and-forth on a plan accumulates spend before any change is written.

See the [prompting guide](/studio-assistant/prompting) for patterns that produce good results in fewer turns.
