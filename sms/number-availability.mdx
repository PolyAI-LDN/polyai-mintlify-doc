Before deploying 2-way SMS, confirm that Twilio supports your target country and choose the right number type. This page covers availability, throughput (MPS), provisioning lead times, and links to each country's regulatory guidelines.

## Number types

| Type | Best for | Throughput | Lead time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Short code** (5–6 digits) | High-volume campaigns and transactional messaging. Highest throughput and best deliverability. | 1,000–3,000 MPS (US/CA) | 6–16 weeks (carrier approval required) |
| **Long code** (standard 10-digit or international format) | Conversational 2-way SMS, lower volume use cases. | 10–225 MPS depending on country | Immediate to private offering |

<Tip>
For US and Canada, long codes are capped at **225 MPS total** across AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon (75 MPS per carrier). If you need higher throughput, apply for a short code.
</Tip>

## Compliance requirements

### US and Canada — A2P 10DLC

US and Canadian long code SMS requires [A2P 10DLC registration](/sms/introduction#a2p-10dlc-registration-us-and-canada). Without it, carriers block messages entirely.

For short code campaigns:
- **US**: Follow the [CTIA Short Code Monitoring Handbook](https://www.ctia.org/) and [Messaging Principles and Best Practices](https://www.twilio.com/docs/messaging/compliance/a2p-10dlc).
- **Canada**: Follow the [Canadian Wireless Telecommunications Association (txt.ca)](https://txt.ca/) guidelines.
- Both US and CA short code applications require a screenshot or mock-up of the opt-in process.
- US short codes require the **EIN** and contact information for the brand associated with the short code.

### United Kingdom

- Follow the [UK Mobile Network Operator Code of Practice](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/guidelines/gb/sms).
- Short code provisioning takes **10–16 weeks** and goes through a separate carrier approval process.
- Long codes are available immediately through the Twilio Console.

### EU countries

Each EU country has its own regulatory framework. Check the regulation links in the tables above for country-specific requirements. Key considerations:
- Some countries (France, Italy) require a **Private Offering** arrangement with Twilio for number provisioning.
- GDPR applies to all EU SMS communications — ensure you have appropriate consent mechanisms.
- Opt-out handling must comply with local telecommunications regulations.

## Pre-launch compliance check

Before going live with 2-way SMS, use the [A2P Campaign Pre-Scanner](https://www.a2pcheck.com/) to validate your campaign setup against carrier requirements.

## Short code provisioning

Short codes offer the highest throughput and best deliverability but require carrier approval:

1. **Apply through Twilio Console** — submit your use case, message samples, and opt-in flow.
2. **Carrier review** — takes 6–16 weeks depending on country. US/CA short codes go through CTIA; UK short codes go through the Mobile Network Operators.
3. **Payment** — Twilio charges automatically once a specialist approves your application (via invoice or credit card on file).

<Warning>
**ISV customers**: If you are provisioning a short code on behalf of a client, you must provide the client's EIN and contact information (not your own) as the brand owner of the short code.
</Warning>
