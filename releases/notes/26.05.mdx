The **May 2026** PolyAI Agent Studio release expands **PolyScore to webchat**, so you can measure conversation quality on equal footing across voice and chat. It also ships a redesigned **Conversations** workspace built for high-volume review, deeper diagnostics on every transcript, and a one-click **Tripleseat** integration for restaurant agents.

Expand the items below for details:

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="PolyScore now scores webchat" icon="star" id="polyscore-webchat">

![polyscore-webchat](/images/release-notes/2605/polyscore-webchat.png)

This is a step change for omni-channel agents: [PolyScore](/analytics/polyscore) now applies to **webchat sessions** as well as voice calls. You no longer have to fall back on hand-built metrics or manual review to assess chat quality – every eligible webchat session gets the same automated 0–10 rating and per-dimension breakdown you already use for voice.

**What's new:**
- **Webchat coverage** – every eligible webchat session is scored automatically against the same dimensions (Conversation quality, Task success, Customer experience) used for voice. The 0–10 scale and color coding (green / amber / red) are identical.
- **Compare voice and chat side by side** – PolyScore is now a like-for-like quality metric across channels, so dashboards, sampling, and QA queues work the same way for both. Each [Conversations](/analytics/conversations/review) scope (Voice, Webchat) shows its own sortable PolyScore column.
- **Same surfaces, both channels** – PolyScore appears in the Conversations table, the **Scores** tab of the review side panel, the home page trend chart, the [Conversations API](/api-reference/conversations/introduction), and as a sampling criterion in [Smart Analyst](/smart-analyst/introduction) – for voice and webchat alike.
- **Refreshed rubric** – the scoring rubric was reworked to remove dimensions that were skewing scores, giving you cleaner, more comparable numbers across the board.

See [PolyScore](/analytics/polyscore) for the full scoring model, eligibility rules, and interpretation guidance.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Conversations workspace revamp" icon="comments" id="conversations-revamp">

![conv-review-side-panel](/images/analytics/conv-review-side-panel.png)

The Conversations page is now organized around channel scopes, saved views, and an integrated review side panel – purpose-built for analyzing conversations at scale.

**What's new:**
- **Voice and Webchat scopes** – the page opens with separate tabs for **Voice** and **Webchat**. Each scope has its own column layout (webchat hides voice-only fields like direction, outbound call status, and CSAT) and its own saved views.
- **Saved views** – save filter and column combinations as **Custom Views** that appear as pinned tabs above the table. Built-in **System Views** (Production and Test) cover the two most common starting points.
- **Private views** – Custom Views are private to your account. Use **Share filters as link** (<kbd>⌘ L</kbd> / <kbd>Ctrl L</kbd>) to share a filter configuration with teammates. Recipients can apply the filters temporarily or save them as their own view.
- **Duplicate, rename, set as default** – manage views from the three-dot menu on every pinned tab. Shortcuts: <kbd>F2</kbd> to rename, <kbd>⌘ D</kbd> / <kbd>Ctrl D</kbd> to duplicate.
- **Integrated review side panel** – clicking a row opens a **Conversation Review** side panel with four tabs (**Transcription / Scores / Details / Custom metrics**). The panel header shows the contact, timestamp, channel type, and language. Sorting, filters, and your place in the list stay put.

See [Conversation review](/analytics/conversations/review) and the new [Views](/analytics/conversations/views) page for the full walkthrough.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Diagnosis as a toggle group" icon="stethoscope" id="diagnosis-toggle-group">

![diagnosis-toggle-group](/images/analytics/conversations-tool-call.png)

The Diagnosis dropdown is now a **toggle group** on the Transcription tab, so you can layer multiple debugging views on top of the transcript at the same time.

**What's new:**
- **Layered toggles** – switch Conversation variables, Flows and steps, Tool calls, Topic citations, Sources, Transcript corrections, Turn latency, Interruptions, Variants, and Logs on or off independently. Choices persist as you move between conversations.
- **Logs layer** – surfaces structured `conv.log.info()`, `conv.log.warning()`, `conv.log.error()`, and `conv.log_api_response()` output from your functions directly under the relevant turn. See [Conversation log](/tools/classes/conv-log).
- **Sources panel** – clicking a Source tag opens an inline preview of the retrieved content with **Open in Knowledge** to jump straight to the source file.

See [Diagnosis](/analytics/conversations/diagnosis) for every available layer.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Tripleseat integration" icon="champagne-glasses" id="tripleseat-integration">

Connect PolyAI to **Tripleseat** to capture event and large-party leads from voice and webchat conversations – without writing custom code.

**What's new:**
- **One-click connection** – on the Integrations page in Agent Studio, find **Tripleseat** and click **Connect** to authorize PolyAI through OAuth 2.0.
- **Large-party handling** – set a max party number in your site settings. When a caller asks for a party larger than that, the agent collects event details and creates a lead in Tripleseat instead of attempting a standard reservation.
- **Multi-channel capture** – the same lead capture works on **voice** (callers speak event details), **webchat** (a guided conversation), and **SMS** (for contact details that are hard to capture over voice).
- **Restaurant template support** – projects built from the PLG restaurant template can connect Tripleseat directly from Agent Studio. Other projects continue to be supported through Managed Services.

See [Tripleseat](/integrations/tripleseat) for the full setup guide.
