## What we'd show

A live demo, not a deck-only pitch. Driven by the design principles in [design principles](design-principles.md). At minimum, the demo must show:

1. A user asking a support question conversationally and getting walked to a solution (not a link).
2. A user uploading a screenshot of an error, and the agent reading it and continuing the troubleshooting.
3. A user switching language mid-conversation and the agent following.
4. A user the agent can't help, getting offered a scheduled callback — and the agent showing that the human will see the conversation context.

That's the four headline capabilities Christopher named. Anything beyond is a stretch goal.

## Open work items (pre-pitch)

- **Quicken discovery.** What's their current support page? Which languages? What's their FAQ / help content situation? Do they have human support agents and a scheduling system? Owner: TBC. See [Quicken](quicken.md).
- **Demo content.** What knowledge is the agent answering from? Real Quicken-shaped content (best), generic personal-finance content (acceptable), or PolyAI's own `/maintain/` content as a placeholder (weakest). Owner: TBC.
- **Prototype tech stack.** Embed [docs.poly.ai AI-assisted search](concept-docs-polyai-ai-search.md) for speed, or build a custom agent? Owner: engineering, TBC.
- **Deck owner.** Who owns the slides that wrap the demo? Frances?
- **Pitch presenter(s).** Frances + Christopher? Frances + a designer?

## What we should not promise on 6 May

- A finished product timeline. We don't know enough about Quicken's setup to commit to one.
- Specific accuracy rates on agent answers. We haven't measured anything yet.
- Specific languages. Until Quicken confirms which their users want, demoing a language switch is generic; promising language X by date Y is premature.

## After the pitch

Whatever happens, file the lessons here. Quicken won't be the only customer this is pitched to — the next one starts from this wiki, not from zero.
