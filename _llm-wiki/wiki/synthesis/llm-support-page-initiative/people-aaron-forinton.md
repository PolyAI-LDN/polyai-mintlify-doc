# Aaron Forinton

PolyAI Technical Writer. Owner of `docs.poly.ai` and the canonical authority on what content exists, where, and what shape it's in.

## Position on this initiative

Pragmatic enabler. On 2026-04-27 he flagged two PolyAI-owned assets that could short-cut the prototype:

1. **The [docs.poly.ai AI-assisted search](concept-docs-polyai-ai-search.md) is embeddable.** Any front-end dev can drop it into another environment to give users an LLM experience over PolyAI's docs immediately. Useful as a fast-path demo.
2. **The [`/maintain/` content](concept-maintain-content.md)** under docs.poly.ai is "designed less to be human-readable and more to solve maintenance related issues." That makes it the closest thing PolyAI already has to an agent-readable knowledge base. Fidelity could be brushed up.

He also pointed out that if the team wants a **Poly widget** specifically (rather than the embedded docs search), the existing maintenance content is a starting point for the `.md` files that would feed it.

## What this means for the project

- Aaron is the right person to pull in for the **knowledge layer** of any prototype: what content exists, what shape it's in, what would need rewriting.
- "Brush up the fidelity" is a real cost we should scope before promising Quicken anything specific. See [Open questions](../open-questions.md).
