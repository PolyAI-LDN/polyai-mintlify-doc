The Open Platform is the self-serve way to try Agent Studio. Sign up with an email address, describe what you want, and [Studio Assistant](/studio-assistant/introduction) scaffolds a working voice agent – flows, knowledge base, voice, integrations – in under 10 minutes. Embed a [PolyPhone widget](/polyphone/introduction) on your site or share the agent via a WebRTC link. No contract, no sales call to start.

The product is still **Agent Studio**. The Open Platform is the open version of it: same platform, same Raven LLM, same infrastructure that runs at scale for enterprise customers.

<Note>
**60-day free trial.** Sign up at [studio.us.poly.ai](https://studio.us.poly.ai/) with any email address – no SSO required. You get 60 days of free access with no billing or metering. If you don't move to a paid plan at the end of the trial, your agents are deactivated rather than deleted, so you can pick them back up later.
</Note>

### Sidebar at a glance

The Open Platform sidebar is a focused subset of the full Agent Studio sidebar. Items not on the Open Platform stay visible in some places but are dimmed and non-interactive on hover; clicking them takes you to a page with a banner explaining what's needed to enable them.

| Section | Items |
|---|---|
| **Build** | Agent · Knowledge · Flows · Tools |
| **Configure** | General · Agent voice · PolyPhone · Numbers · Integrations |
| **Analytics** | Dashboards · Conversations · Smart Analyst |
| **Studio Assistant** | The chat panel above the sidebar (the AI builder) |
| **Deployments** | Environments · Project history · Widgets *(PolyPhone only)* |

Build and Configure modules are visible and primarily read-only on the Open Platform – you make changes through Studio Assistant rather than by editing flows, topics, or settings directly. Everything not in the table above – telephony routing, SMS, handoffs, Agent Analysis, custom metrics and dashboards, source hub, real-time configuration, language hub, audio management, advanced safety – is enterprise-only and not surfaced in the Open Platform sidebar. Those pages stay browseable in these docs; in the [Open Platform docs view](#open-platform-docs-view) the page content greys out behind a banner so you can read it without acting on it.

## What's enterprise-only

These features sit on the enterprise side of the platform. You can read about them in these docs, but they aren't available on the Open Platform – they need an enterprise contract for the integration work, contracted infrastructure, or capacity that powers them.

If you try to connect an enterprise-only integration in Studio, the product tells you directly:

![Integration Restricted modal showing Twilio is not enabled on self-serve accounts, with Close and Contact Sales buttons](/images/platform/enterprise-restricted-modal.png)

### Still visible in the Open Platform sidebar

A couple of items stay in the nav but are dimmed and non-interactive on hover. Click one and the page opens with a banner explaining what enterprise enablement is needed.

| Capability | What you see |
|---|---|
| **Numbers** (under Configure) | The page opens with an enterprise banner. PSTN telephony provisioning is contracted per-tenant on the enterprise cluster. |
| **Smart Analyst** (under Analytics) | The page opens, and advanced queries that depend on services not deployed on the Open Platform cluster show an enterprise banner instead of running. |

### Not in the Open Platform sidebar

| Capability | Why it's enterprise-only |
|---|---|
| **Outbound and inbound telephony** | Live PSTN routing is contracted per-tenant on the enterprise cluster. The Open Platform runs on a separate cluster without telephony at launch. Bring-your-own Twilio numbers may be possible via the ADK on enterprise accounts. |
| **Live human handoff** | Requires SIP trunking to your contact-center platform (Genesys, Five9, Salesforce Voice, etc.). |
| **SMS and SMS handoff** | Requires a provisioned SMS provider integration. |
| **Webchat widget** | The Open Platform launches with the PolyPhone voice widget only. Webchat parity is on the post-launch roadmap. |
| **Agent Analysis** | Standalone Agent Analysis tab is enterprise-only. |
| **Real-time configuration** | Schema-driven runtime config (Python schema, multi-environment values). |
| **Custom metrics, CSAT, custom dashboards** | Bespoke analytics that need account-level configuration. The standard dashboard is included. |
| **Enterprise safety filters** | Red-teamed safety rules and custom guardrails layered on top of the platform's standard guardrails. |
| **Source hub (connected knowledge)** | Live document syncing from Confluence, Sharepoint, Google Drive, etc. |
| **Audio management** | Custom recorded prompts and voice cloning. |
| **Language hub** | Multi-language agent variants with shared content. |
| **Advanced speech recognition and response control** | ASR tuning and response-shaping settings are reserved for the enterprise tier. |
| **Workspace API keys** | Use [personal access tokens](/secrets/api-keys) and the [Agents API](/api-reference/agents/introduction) on the Open Platform instead. |

## Building locally with the ADK

The [PolyAI ADK](/extend/adk) (`polyai-adk` on PyPI) lets you pull your project as YAML and Python files, edit with any tool, and push changes back. It works for self-serve accounts the same way it works for enterprise. Sign up at [studio.us.poly.ai](https://studio.us.poly.ai/), generate a [personal access token](/secrets/api-keys), and run `poly init`.

```bash
pip install polyai-adk
export POLY_ADK_KEY=<your-personal-access-token>
poly init
```

Full installation, authentication, and command reference at [polyai.github.io/adk](https://polyai.github.io/adk/).

## Moving to enterprise

When you need telephony, real-time configuration, contracted SLAs, or any of the other enterprise-only capabilities listed above, you can carry the project you built on the Open Platform across to an enterprise cluster.

- Export and import are built into the ADK: `poly pull` your project, then `poly push` to the enterprise account.
- [Request a demo](https://poly.ai/request-a-demo) when you're ready to discuss an enterprise contract.
- Existing enterprise contracts get the full sidebar with no dimmed entries.

## Security

The Open Platform sits behind AWS WAF and CloudFront. Every account is isolated – usage limits, conversation data, and credentials are scoped per-account, and tenant context is derived from the signed JWT issued at sign-in. There is no cross-account access.

Sensitive credentials (API keys, OAuth client secrets) belong in the [Secrets Vault](/secrets/introduction). Functions retrieve them at runtime; they never sit in code, logs, or version history.

<Note>
**Hover behaviour for dimmed sidebar items.** Dimmed sidebar entries hold their muted state on hover – they don't darken or animate like active entries. The link itself is still clickable, but the hover affordance signals "you can read this, you can't act on it" before you commit to the click.
</Note>

### How to exit

You can leave the Open Platform docs view at any time in three ways:

- Click the **Exit Open Platform view** pill floating in the top-right corner of any docs page.
- If you land on an enterprise page in the Open Platform view, click **Exit Open Platform view** in the page-top banner.
- Come back to this page and click **Exit Open Platform view**.
