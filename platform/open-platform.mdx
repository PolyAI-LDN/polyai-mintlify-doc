The Open platform is the self-serve way to try Agent Studio. Sign up with an email address, describe what you want, and [Studio Assistant](/studio-assistant/introduction) scaffolds a working voice agent – flows, knowledge base, voice, integrations – in under 10 minutes. Embed a [PolyPhone widget](/polyphone/introduction) on your site or share the agent via a WebRTC link. No contract, no sales call to start.

The product is still **Agent Studio**. The Open platform is the open version of it: same platform, same Raven LLM, same infrastructure that runs at scale for enterprise customers.

<Note>
**60-day free trial.** Sign up at [studio.poly.ai](https://studio.poly.ai/) with any email address – no SSO required. You get 60 days of free access with no billing or metering. If you don't move to a paid plan at the end of the trial, your agents are deactivated rather than deleted, so you can pick them back up later.
</Note>

## Building locally with the ADK

The [PolyAI ADK](/extend/adk) (`polyai-adk` on PyPI) lets you pull your project as YAML and Python files, edit with any tool, and push changes back. It works for self-serve accounts the same way it works for enterprise. Sign up at [studio.poly.ai](https://studio.poly.ai/), generate a [personal access token](/secrets/api-keys), and run `poly init`.

```bash
pip install polyai-adk
export POLY_ADK_KEY=<your-personal-access-token>
poly init
```

Full installation, authentication, and command reference at [polyai.github.io/adk](https://polyai.github.io/adk/).

## Moving to enterprise

When you need telephony, real-time configuration, contracted SLAs, or any of the other enterprise-only capabilities listed above, you can carry the project you built on the Open platform across to an enterprise cluster.

- Export and import are built into the ADK: `poly pull` your project, then `poly push` to the enterprise account.
- [Request a demo](https://poly.ai/request-a-demo) when you're ready to discuss an enterprise contract.
- Existing enterprise contracts get the full sidebar with no dimmed entries.

## Security and hosting

The Open platform runs on a dedicated cluster, separate from the enterprise infrastructure. It sits behind AWS WAF and CloudFront. Every account is isolated – usage limits, conversation data, and credentials are scoped per-account, and tenant context is derived from the signed JWT issued at sign-in. There is no cross-account access.

The Open platform runs in the **US (`us-1`) region**. Multi-region availability (`euw-1`, `uk-1`) is part of the enterprise tier; carry your project across via `poly pull` → `poly push` when you need regional residency.

Sensitive credentials (API keys, OAuth client secrets) belong in the [Secrets Vault](/secrets/introduction). Functions retrieve them at runtime; they never sit in code, logs, or version history.

<Note>
**Hover behavior for dimmed sidebar items.** Dimmed sidebar entries hold their muted state on hover – they don't darken or animate like active entries. The cursor stays as the default arrow rather than changing to a pointer, so the entry visually reads as inert. The link itself is still clickable, but the hover affordance signals "you can read this, you can't act on it" before you commit to the click.
</Note>
