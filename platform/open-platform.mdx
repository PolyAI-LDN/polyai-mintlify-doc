Agent Builder is Agent Studio without the contract. Sign up with any email at [studio.poly.ai](https://studio.poly.ai/), describe what you want to [Studio Assistant](/studio-assistant/introduction), and you get a working voice agent you can test in the browser and embed on your site. The first agent typically takes ten minutes.

It runs on the same Raven model and infrastructure that powers enterprise deployments. There's no separate sandbox mode and no toy version. What you build is production-ready voice AI.

<Note>
**60-day free trial.** Sign up at [studio.poly.ai](https://studio.poly.ai/) with any email address. You get 60 days of free access with no billing or metering. If you don't move to a paid plan at the end of the trial, your agents are deactivated rather than deleted, so you can pick them back up later.
</Note>

## Your first 10 minutes

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up">
    Open [studio.poly.ai](https://studio.poly.ai/) and create an account with any email address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the agent to Studio Assistant">
    Tell [Studio Assistant](/studio-assistant/introduction) what the agent should do in plain language. Paste a URL or a document if you want it to seed [Knowledge](/managed-topics/introduction) from real content.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test and embed">
    Call the agent in the browser the moment the plan applies. When it sounds right, drop the [PolyPhone widget](/polyphone/introduction) script tag on your site.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What's included

| Feature | What it does |
|---|---|
| [Studio Assistant](/studio-assistant/introduction) | Describe an agent in natural language. It scaffolds flows, topics, voice, and integrations on a branch you can review before merging. |
| [PolyPhone widget](/polyphone/introduction) | A WebRTC voice widget you embed on any site. End-to-end browser audio. Voice-only on Agent Builder. |
| [Managed topics](/managed-topics/introduction) | The agent's knowledge layer. Auto-populated from your URL or documents, editable in Studio Assistant or in the UI. |
| [No-code flows](/flows/no-code/introduction) | Multi-step conversations built visually with Default steps and entity extraction. Booking, verification, routing, FAQ — all without writing code. |
| [Voice library](/voice/introduction) | The standard PolyAI voice catalog. Configurable per agent. |
| [Marketplace integrations](/integrations/introduction) | Microsoft, Zoom, Zendesk, Slack, and web search via Paragon. Plus any MCP server. |
| [Conversations and analytics](/analytics/conversations/introduction) | Every transcript and recording, plus the standard dashboard. Smart Analyst is folded into Studio Assistant. |
| [ADK](/extend/adk) | `polyai-adk` on PyPI. Pull your project as YAML and Python, edit anywhere, push it back. Authenticate with a [personal access token](/secrets/api-keys). |

## Usage limits

Studio Assistant has a fair-use limit so usage stays predictable. Size and reset cadence are set on your account; the indicator next to the message input shows your current usage. See [Usage and limits](/studio-assistant/usage-and-limits).

## Security and hosting

Agent Builder runs on its own dedicated cluster, separate from enterprise infrastructure. Your account is isolated: conversations, configuration, and credentials are only visible to you. No cross-account access.

Agent Builder is hosted in the US. EU and UK hosting are enterprise.

Sensitive credentials (API keys, OAuth client secrets) belong in the [Secrets Vault](/secrets/introduction). Functions retrieve them at runtime, so they don't end up in your code, logs, or version history.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What happens at the end of the 60-day free trial?" icon="calendar-xmark">
    Your PolyAI representative shares paid-plan options before your free period ends. If you don't move to a paid plan, your agents are **deactivated, not deleted**. They stop handling calls but stay on your account so you can pick them back up later. Sign-up data, transcripts, and configuration are retained while the account is on file.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this enterprise-grade or a sandbox?" icon="circle-check">
    It's the same platform. Every agent on Agent Builder runs on [Raven](/agent-settings/raven), PolyAI's proprietary voice model, on the same infrastructure that handles enterprise deployments. What you build here is production-ready voice AI.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What about phone numbers and telephony?" icon="phone">
    PSTN telephony is enterprise. PolyPhone (WebRTC voice in the browser) is how voice is delivered on Agent Builder. Enterprise accounts can bring their own Twilio numbers via the ADK.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my data?" icon="shield">
    Your conversations, recordings, knowledge, and credentials are only visible to your account. There's no cross-account access. If you ever want your data deleted entirely rather than just deactivated, your PolyAI representative can do that for you.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Local development with the ADK

The [ADK](/extend/adk) works the same on Agent Builder as it does on enterprise. Pull your project as YAML and Python, edit with any tool, push changes back.

```bash
pip install polyai-adk
export POLY_ADK_KEY=<your-personal-access-token>
poly init
```

Generate the PAT in **Secrets > Personal access tokens**. Full ADK reference at [polyai.github.io/adk](https://polyai.github.io/adk/).

## Moving to enterprise

Your work moves with you. When you need telephony, real-time configuration, contracted SLAs, or any of the enterprise capabilities, `poly pull` the project, then `poly push` onto an enterprise cluster. [Request a demo](https://poly.ai/request-a-demo) when you're ready.
