The Open platform is the self-serve way to try Agent Studio. Sign up with an email address, describe what you want, and [Studio Assistant](/studio-assistant/introduction) scaffolds a working voice agent (flows, knowledge base, voice, integrations) in under 10 minutes. Embed a [PolyPhone widget](/polyphone/introduction) on your site or share the agent via a WebRTC link. No contract, no sales call to start.

The product is still **Agent Studio**. The Open platform is the open version of it: same platform, same Raven LLM, same infrastructure that runs at scale for enterprise customers.

<Note>
**60-day free trial.** Sign up at [studio.poly.ai](https://studio.poly.ai/) with any email address, no SSO required. You get 60 days of free access with no billing or metering. If you don't move to a paid plan at the end of the trial, your agents are deactivated rather than deleted, so you can pick them back up later.
</Note>

### Sidebar at a glance

The Open platform sidebar is a focused subset of the full Agent Studio sidebar. Below is exactly what renders for a self-serve account.

| Section | Items |
|---|---|
| **Home** | Studio Assistant chat panel as the landing surface |
| **Analytics** | The standard dashboard (total conversations, containment, PolyScore overview, cost savings, handle time) |
| **Conversations** | Every conversation transcript and recording |
| **Build** | Agent · Knowledge · Flows · Tools |
| **Configure** | General · Agent voice · PolyPhone · Numbers · Integrations |
| **Deployments** | Sandbox and Live environments |

Build and Configure are visible but mostly read-only on the Open platform. You make changes by asking Studio Assistant rather than editing flows, topics, or settings directly. **Numbers** is the one item that opens with an enterprise banner: PSTN telephony provisioning is contracted per-tenant on the enterprise cluster. Smart Analyst lives inside the Studio Assistant home as a chat panel rather than a standalone tab.

## What's not on the Open platform

The features above are everything you need for a working voice agent. Other features aren't on the Open platform: enterprise-grade telephony, SMS, live human handoffs, custom dashboards and metrics, multi-language hubs, the test suite, multi-region hosting, and the Conversations API. They need contracted infrastructure, account-level setup, or carrier integrations that don't fit a self-serve plan.

If you try to use one of them in Studio, the product says so:

![Integration Restricted modal showing Twilio is not enabled on self-serve accounts, with Close and Contact Sales buttons](/images/platform/enterprise-restricted-modal.png)

These pages still appear in these docs so you can read about them. In the [Open platform docs view](#open-platform-docs-view) they're dimmed in the sidebar, and clicking through opens the page behind a banner that explains what's needed to enable it. When you're ready for any of these capabilities, [request a demo](https://poly.ai/request-a-demo) to talk about an enterprise plan.

## Building locally with the ADK

The [PolyAI ADK](/extend/adk) (`polyai-adk` on PyPI) lets you pull your project as YAML and Python files, edit with any tool, and push changes back. It works for self-serve accounts the same way it works for enterprise. Sign up at [studio.poly.ai](https://studio.poly.ai/), generate a [personal access token](/secrets/api-keys), and run `poly init`.

```bash
pip install polyai-adk
export POLY_ADK_KEY=<your-personal-access-token>
poly init
```

Full installation, authentication, and command reference at [polyai.github.io/adk](https://polyai.github.io/adk/).

## Moving to enterprise

Your work comes with you. When you need telephony, real-time configuration, contracted SLAs, or any of the other enterprise capabilities, use the [ADK](/extend/adk) to `poly pull` your project, then `poly push` it onto an enterprise cluster. [Request a demo](https://poly.ai/request-a-demo) when you're ready to talk about a contract.

## Security and hosting

The Open platform runs on its own dedicated cluster, separate from the enterprise infrastructure. Every account is isolated: your conversations, configuration, and credentials are only visible to your account, and there is no cross-account access.

The Open platform is hosted in the **US**. Multi-region hosting (EU and UK) is part of the enterprise tier.

API keys and other sensitive credentials live in the [Secrets Vault](/secrets/introduction). Functions read them at runtime; they don't end up in your code, logs, or version history.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this enterprise-grade, or a sandbox?" icon="circle-check">
    It's the same platform. Every agent on the Open platform runs on [Raven](/agent-settings/raven), PolyAI's proprietary voice model, on the same infrastructure that handles enterprise deployments. There's no separate sandbox mode. What you build here is production-ready voice AI. The Open platform is hosted in the US; EU and UK hosting are part of the enterprise tier.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What about Smart Analyst, PolyScore, and the analytics tabs?" icon="chart-line">
    Smart Analyst is integrated into the [Studio Assistant](/studio-assistant/introduction) home as a chat panel. Ask things like *"why did containment drop on Tuesday?"* or *"summarize the failed calls"* and the assistant queries your conversation data directly. The standalone Smart Analyst, Agent Analysis, and the full PolyScore breakdown are enterprise. PolyScore still appears as an overview metric on the standard dashboard.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I share my agent without an account?" icon="share-nodes">
    Yes. Every agent gets a shareable WebRTC link from **Deployment > Widget**. Send it to colleagues, prospects, or stakeholders. They call it from any browser, no login required. Use this for demos and internal sign-off before you embed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What about phone numbers and telephony?" icon="phone">
    PSTN telephony is on the enterprise tier. PolyPhone (WebRTC voice in the browser) is how voice is delivered on the Open platform.   <Accordion title="Can I export my agent? Can I move it to enterprise later?" icon="arrow-up-right-from-square">

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my data?" icon="shield">
    Your conversations, recordings, knowledge, and credentials are only visible to your account. There's no cross-account access. If you ever want your data deleted entirely rather than just deactivated, your PolyAI representative can do that for you.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I get help?" icon="circle-question">
    Studio Assistant in **Ask** mode is the fastest way to debug your own agent. It can explain why a topic matched, what a flow does, or why a tool call failed. For platform questions outside your agent, use the **Talk to sales** link inside Studio Assistant or [request a demo](https://poly.ai/request-a-demo).

## Open platform docs view

The Open platform docs view is a focused view of these docs for self-serve users. Enterprise-only entries are dimmed in the sidebar so you can see at a glance what's in the Open platform and what isn't, but every entry stays clickable. Open one and the page content is grayed out and non-interactive behind a banner that names what would be needed to enable it.

<CardGroup cols={2}>

  <Card title="Enterprise pages lock on arrival" icon="lock">

  <Card title="Page-top banner" icon="circle-info">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Persists across pages" icon="bookmark">
    Once you turn it on, the Open platform view stays on across pages and sessions until you click **Open platform — exit**.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
**Hover behavior for dimmed sidebar items.** Dimmed sidebar entries hold their muted state on hover. They don't darken or animate like active entries. The cursor stays as the default arrow rather than changing to a pointer, so the entry visually reads as inert. The link itself is still clickable, but the hover affordance signals "you can read this, you can't act on it" before you commit to the click.
</Note>
