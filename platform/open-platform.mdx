The Open platform is Agent Studio, opened up. Sign up with any email, describe what you want, and [Studio Assistant](/studio-assistant/introduction) scaffolds the agent for you. Test it in the browser the moment it's built. Embed it on your site with a single script tag. No contract. No sales call.

It's the same platform that runs enterprise voice AI at scale. Same Raven model. Same infrastructure. Just opened to anyone who wants to try it.

<Note>
**60-day free trial.** Sign up at [studio.poly.ai](https://studio.poly.ai/) with any email address. You get 60 days of free access with no billing or metering. If you don't move to a paid plan at the end of the trial, your agents are deactivated rather than deleted, so you can pick them back up later.
</Note>

## What isn't

Other things sit on the enterprise side: PSTN telephony, SMS, live human handoffs, custom dashboards and metrics, multi-language hubs, the test suite, multi-region hosting, the Conversations API. They need contracted infrastructure, account-level setup, or carrier integrations that don't fit a self-serve plan.

You can still read about them in these docs. In the Open platform view, they're dimmed in the sidebar; click one and the page opens behind an "Open platform — exit" banner that explains what would be needed to use it.

If you try to use one of them in Studio, the product says so directly:

![Integration Restricted modal showing Twilio is not enabled on self-serve accounts, with Close and Contact Sales buttons](/images/platform/enterprise-restricted-modal.png)

When you're ready for any of these, [request a demo](https://poly.ai/request-a-demo).

## Usage limits

Studio Assistant has a fair-use limit so usage stays predictable. The size and reset cadence are managed on your account; the indicator next to the message input shows where you stand. See [Usage and limits](/studio-assistant/usage-and-limits) for the details.

## Building locally with the ADK

The [ADK](/extend/adk) (`polyai-adk` on PyPI) is the same on the Open platform as on enterprise. Pull your project as YAML and Python, edit anywhere, push it back.

```bash
pip install polyai-adk
export POLY_ADK_KEY=<your-personal-access-token>
poly init
```

Generate the PAT from **Secrets > Personal access tokens** in Studio. Full reference at [polyai.github.io/adk](https://polyai.github.io/adk/).

## Moving to enterprise

Your work comes with you. When you need telephony, real-time configuration, contracted SLAs, or any of the enterprise capabilities, `poly pull` your project, then `poly push` it onto an enterprise cluster. [Request a demo](https://poly.ai/request-a-demo) when you're ready to talk about a contract.

## Security and hosting

The Open platform runs on its own dedicated cluster, separate from the enterprise infrastructure. Your account is isolated: your conversations, configuration, and credentials are only visible to you, and there is no cross-account access.

The Open platform is hosted in the US. EU and UK hosting are part of the enterprise tier.

API keys and other sensitive credentials live in the [Secrets Vault](/secrets/introduction). Functions read them at runtime; they don't end up in your code, logs, or version history.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What happens at the end of the 60-day free trial?" icon="calendar-xmark">
    Your PolyAI representative shares paid-plan options before your free period ends. If you don't move to a paid plan, your agents are **deactivated, not deleted**. They stop handling calls but stay on your account so you can pick them back up later. Sign-up data, transcripts, and configuration are retained while the account is on file.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this enterprise-grade or a sandbox?" icon="circle-check">
    It's the same platform. Every agent on the Open platform runs on [Raven](/agent-settings/raven), PolyAI's proprietary voice model, on the same infrastructure that handles enterprise deployments. What you build here is production-ready voice AI.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What about phone numbers and telephony?" icon="phone">
    PSTN telephony is enterprise. PolyPhone (WebRTC voice in the browser) is how voice is delivered on the Open platform. Enterprise accounts can bring their own Twilio numbers via the ADK.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my data?" icon="shield">
    Your conversations, recordings, knowledge, and credentials are only visible to your account. There's no cross-account access. If you ever want your data deleted entirely rather than just deactivated, your PolyAI representative can do that for you.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
