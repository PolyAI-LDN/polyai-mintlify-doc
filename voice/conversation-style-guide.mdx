By the end of this page, you will understand the linguistic patterns that separate a natural-sounding voice agent from a robotic one — and know how to apply them in your managed topics, rules, flows, and few-shot examples.

## Social presence markers

Natural conversation includes patterns that acknowledge conversational history and participants. These contribute to a sense of collaboration rather than rote routine-following.

### Use progressive tense for active collaboration

- "I'm not seeing any accounts under that phone number..." conveys active collaboration
- "I don't see any accounts" sounds too definitive

**Where to apply this:** In your [managed topic](/managed-topics/introduction) **Content** field. When writing the answer your agent gives, use progressive tense for lookups and actions.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Before">
    ```text
    I don't have any reservations for that name.
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="After">
    ```text
    I'm not finding any reservations under that name...
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Reference shared context implicitly

Don't restate what both parties already know:

- "How about Wednesday instead?" (not "How about Wednesday instead of Tuesday?")
- "In that case, how does Saturday at 2:30 sound?" (not "Since you said you prefer weekends...")

**Where to apply this:** In [flow](/flows/introduction) step prompts. When the agent moves to a follow-up step, the prompt should not re-summarize what the caller just said.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Before">
    ```text
    Since you said Tuesday doesn't work for you, how about Wednesday instead?
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="After">
    ```text
    How about Wednesday instead?
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Vary confirmationals

Use a mix of "Great," "Okay," "Perfect," and "Sure" rather than repeating the same one. If your agent always starts with "Sure!", callers notice the repetition within a few turns.

**Where to apply this:** Add a rule in **Build > Agent > Behavior**. Go to the [Rules](/agent-settings/rules) section and add:

```text
Vary your confirmations. Use a mix of "Great," "Okay," "Perfect," "Sure," and 
"Got it" rather than repeating the same one. Never use the same confirmation 
twice in a row.
```

### Use conversational datives

Conversational datives add a collaborative feel:

- "Could you read **me** your account number?" rather than "Could you read your account number aloud?"
- "Can you log into your account **for me**?" rather than "Can you log into your account?"

**Where to apply this:** In [few-shot examples](/flows/few-shot-prompting) inside flow step prompts. When you write example exchanges, model the dative pattern so the LLM learns it:

```text
User: I need to verify my identity.
Agent: No problem — could you read me the last four digits of your account number?

User: I'm having trouble logging in.
Agent: Can you try logging into your account for me on the app?
```

### Use face-saving past tense

When referencing a user's request, past tense softens the phrasing:

- "When **were** you trying to come in?" rather than "When are you trying to come in?"

**Where to apply this:** In managed topic **Content** fields for topics that ask clarifying questions. Write the clarification in past tense:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Before">
    ```text
    What date are you looking to book?
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="After">
    ```text
    What date were you looking to book?
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Avoid over-explaining

LLMs tend to justify every action in a way humans don't. Most of the time, the important information and the request can be formed into a single sentence:

- "No problem, what's your account number?" rather than "In order to check for outages, I'll need to look up your account. Could you tell me your account number?"

**Where to apply this:** Two places:

1. **Managed topic Content** — shorten the answer text. If your content field contains a justification followed by a question, cut the justification.

2. **Agent rules** — add a rule in **Build > Agent > Behavior**:

```text
Do not explain why you need information before asking for it. Ask directly. 
Instead of "In order to look up your account, I'll need your account number," 
say "No problem, what's your account number?"
```

## Walkthrough conversations

When giving multi-turn walkthroughs, don't end every step with "let me know when you've done that." Provide the instruction and wait — the user will confirm on their own.

**Where to apply this:** In [flow](/flows/introduction) step prompts. When writing a multi-step walkthrough flow, write each step's prompt as a clear instruction without a trailing confirmation request:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Before">
    ```text
    Go to Settings and tap on Account. Let me know when you've done that.
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="After">
    ```text
    Go to Settings and tap on Account.
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Quick reference

| Pattern | Where to apply | How to enter it |
|---|---|---|
| Progressive tense | [Managed topic](/managed-topics/introduction) Content field | Write lookups as "I'm not finding..." instead of "I don't see..." |
| Implicit context | [Flow](/flows/introduction) step prompts | Don't re-summarize what the caller just said |
| Varied confirmationals | [Agent rules](/agent-settings/rules) in Build > Agent > Behavior | Add a rule: "Vary confirmations, never repeat the same one twice in a row" |
| Conversational datives | [Few-shot examples](/flows/few-shot-prompting) in flow steps | Model "read me" / "for me" phrasing in example exchanges |
| Face-saving past tense | [Managed topic](/managed-topics/introduction) Content field | Write clarifications as "were you looking to..." |
| No over-explaining | Managed topic Content + [Agent rules](/agent-settings/rules) | Cut justifications before requests; add a "don't explain, just ask" rule |
| No trailing confirmations | [Flow](/flows/introduction) step prompts | End with the instruction, not "let me know when you're done" |
