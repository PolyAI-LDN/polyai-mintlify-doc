Most issues can be resolved without contacting support. Start with the steps below, and reach out if you still need help.

## Troubleshoot first

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check common issues">
    Review the [common issues](/learn/maintain/common-issues) table for quick fixes to frequent problems like missing edits, failed handoffs, or broken imports.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the FAQ">
    The [FAQ](/troubleshoot/faq) covers questions about topics, rules, actions, and agent configuration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Diagnose the conversation">
    Use [conversation diagnosis](/analytics/conversations/diagnosis) to inspect function calls, flows, topic citations, and turn latency for a specific call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your environment">
    Confirm you're looking at the right [environment](/environments-and-versions/introduction). Changes saved in Sandbox aren't live until promoted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Contact support

If the steps above don't resolve your issue, email the PolyAI support team at **[support@poly.ai](mailto:support@poly.ai)**.

Include the following in your message:

- **Project link** — a URL to the relevant project in Studio
- **Environment** — Sandbox, Pre-release, or Live
- **Call ID** — from the [Conversations](/analytics/conversations/review) page, if applicable
- **Description** — what you expected vs. what happened
- **Steps to reproduce** — how to trigger the issue

<Tip>
Including a call ID helps the support team investigate faster.
</Tip>

## Choose the right priority

When you raise a support ticket, select the priority level that matches your situation. Using the correct priority ensures the right response time and avoids delays for critical issues.

### P1 — Urgent

**You cannot access or use your virtual agent at all.** The service is completely unavailable or critically impaired, causing immediate and significant business impact.

Resolution target: **4 hours**

Examples:
- Your virtual assistant is not picking up any calls
- All callers are being dropped immediately after connecting
- The phone number rings but never connects to the agent
- Complete outage across all skill groups

<Tip>
For emergencies outside working hours, use the 24/7 phone line for the fastest response. During working hours, email and the support portal also work for P1 issues.
</Tip>

### P2 — High

**A major piece of functionality is not working as expected.** The virtual agent is only partially usable, with significant impact to key capabilities.

Resolution target: **8 hours**

Examples:
- A reservation or booking flow is failing for all callers
- Transfers to your most-used queue are not completing
- A core intent (e.g. "cancel my order") is consistently not recognised across many calls
- A critical integration (e.g. CRM lookup) is returning errors for every call

### P3 — Medium

**An error or functional issue is affecting part of the virtual agent.** The issue causes moderate business impact and may allow a workaround.

Examples:
- A few customers have reported the agent misunderstood them on a specific question
- An intent occasionally fails but works when tested
- A single, lower-volume skill group is behaving unexpectedly
- You've spotted an isolated wrong response in call recordings

### P4 — Low

**The issue does not significantly impact business operations.** It may relate to minor defects, non-critical functionality issues, or general service requests.

Examples:
- You want to update your opening hours or add a public holiday closure
- You'd like to change the wording of a greeting or FAQ response
- You have a question about a metric in the analytics dashboard
- You want to add a new intent or question to the agent

<Info>
P3 and P4 issues do not have a defined resolution time SLA. They are addressed in priority order after P1 and P2 issues.
</Info>

## What to expect after you raise a ticket

Once you submit a support request, here's how the process works:

### Response and resolution times

| Priority | First response | Resolution target |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **P1 — Urgent** | Immediate | 4 hours |
| **P2 — High** | Within SLA window | 8 hours |
| **P3 — Medium** | Within SLA window | As soon as possible |
| **P4 — Low** | Within SLA window | As soon as possible |

### Ticket statuses

Your ticket moves through these statuses as the support team works on it:

| Status | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| **Open** | Your ticket has been received and is in the queue |
| **In Progress** | A support engineer is actively working on your issue |
| **Waiting for Customer** | The support team needs more information from you — check your email for questions |
| **Resolved** | The issue has been fixed or the question answered |

<Tip>
If your ticket shows "Waiting for Customer," reply with the requested information to move it back into the active queue. Response SLAs pause while waiting for your reply.
</Tip>

### Support availability

PolyAI support is available **every day**, including weekends and public holidays, for P1 and P2 issues.

## Report a bug

If you believe you've found a product bug:

1. Reproduce the issue and capture screenshots or relevant logs.
2. Note your browser (e.g. Chrome, Safari) and environment (Sandbox, Pre-release, or Live).
3. Email [support@poly.ai](mailto:support@poly.ai) with the details above.

<Warning>
Always test in [Sandbox](/environments-and-versions/introduction) before reporting issues found in production. Some problems are caused by unpromoted changes.
</Warning>
