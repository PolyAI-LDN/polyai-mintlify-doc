Answers to questions about setting your agent's personality, tone, and role. This FAQ emphasizes using specific examples and rules rather than generic personality instructions. See [Agent](/agent-settings/agent) for full documentation.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I set the agent's personality and tone?" icon="face-smile">
    Personality and role are configured in **Build > Agent**. The greeting is configured per-channel under **Channels > Voice > Voice configuration** (or **Channels > Chat > Chat configuration**), not on the Agent page.

    The built-in personality tags (`Polite`, `Kind`, `Funny`, `Energetic`, `Calm`, `Thoughtful`) are simply inserted as adjectives into the system prompt – e.g. _"You are a polite, kind \[role]."_. Selecting **Other** replaces the joined adjectives with your free-form string. There's no hidden behavior tied to specific words – they're literal prompt content.

    For detailed tone control, use [global rules](/agent-settings/rules) with **specific examples** of how the agent should respond. Single-sentence instructions like "Be professional" are less reliable than concrete examples:

    **Instead of:** "Be friendly and helpful."

    **Use a rule like:** "When greeting the customer, use their name if available. Example: 'Hi Sarah, thanks for calling \[Brand]. How can I help you today?'"

    <Tip>
      Always test tone changes in [sandbox](/environments-and-versions/introduction) with adversarial inputs before promoting.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is defining a role necessary?" icon="user-tag">
    It helps the agent stay focused. Add this in the [Agent](/agent-settings/agent) section or as a global rule.

    **Examples:**
    - "You are a virtual agent for \[Brand], focused on customer support."
    - "You are a helpful hotel concierge, focused on resolving customer problems and managing reservations."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
